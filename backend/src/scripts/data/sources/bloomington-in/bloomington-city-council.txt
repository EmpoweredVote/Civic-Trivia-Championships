City of Bloomington - City Council

Bloomington Common Council

The Bloomington Common Council is the legislative body of the City of Bloomington, Indiana. The Council is composed of nine (9) members elected from three council districts.

Council Structure
- 9 council members total
- Three (3) council members elected from each of three (3) geographic districts
- Council members serve 4-year terms
- Elections held in odd-numbered years (municipal elections)
- The Council elects its own President and President Pro Tempore

Council Districts
The City of Bloomington is divided into three council districts:
- District 1: Northwest portion of Bloomington
- District 2: Northeast and central portions of Bloomington
- District 3: South and southeast portions of Bloomington

City Council Responsibilities
The Bloomington Common Council:
- Adopts the city budget
- Sets property tax rates
- Passes city ordinances
- Approves zoning changes and development projects
- Confirms mayoral appointments to boards and commissions
- Reviews and approves contracts
- Sets municipal policy

City Council Meetings
The Bloomington Common Council meets regularly throughout the year. Regular meetings are open to the public. Meeting agendas and minutes are posted on the city website. Citizens can address the council during public comment periods.

City Government Overview
Bloomington operates under a mayor-council form of government. The Mayor serves as chief executive and the Common Council serves as the legislative branch.

The Mayor of Bloomington
The Mayor of Bloomington is the chief executive of the city. The Mayor is elected to a four-year term. Kerry Thomson was elected Mayor in November 2023 and took office on January 1, 2024. Mayor Thomson's term runs through December 31, 2027.

City Clerk
The Bloomington City Clerk is an elected position responsible for maintaining city records, administering city elections, and serving as clerk to the Common Council.

City Departments
The City of Bloomington has numerous departments including:
- Bloomington Police Department
- Bloomington Fire Department
- City Utilities (water and wastewater services)
- Parks and Recreation Department
- Planning and Transportation Department
- Public Works Department
- Economic and Sustainable Development
- City Controller's Office
- City Legal Department
- Information and Technology Services

City Boards and Commissions
Bloomington has numerous boards and commissions appointed by the Mayor and confirmed by the Council, including:
- Plan Commission
- Board of Zoning Appeals
- Parks and Recreation Commission
- Utilities Service Board
- Bloomington Transit Board

Bloomington's History
Bloomington was incorporated as a town in 1818 and as a city in 1876. Bloomington is the county seat of Monroe County, Indiana. Indiana University was established in Bloomington in 1820.

City Charter and Code
Bloomington operates under a city charter. The Bloomington Municipal Code contains the city's ordinances and regulations.
