Indiana Governor's Office - Official State Website

Indiana Governor

The Governor of Indiana is the chief executive of the State of Indiana. The Governor is elected to a four-year term and may serve two consecutive terms.

Current Governor
Mike Braun was elected as the 52nd Governor of Indiana in November 2024 and took office on January 13, 2025. Governor Braun previously served as a U.S. Senator from Indiana (2019-2025). His term runs through January 2029.

Lieutenant Governor
The Lieutenant Governor of Indiana serves as the second-highest executive officer in Indiana state government. The Lieutenant Governor is elected on a ticket with the Governor and presides over the Indiana Senate.

Responsibilities of the Governor
The Governor of Indiana:
- Signs or vetoes legislation passed by the Indiana General Assembly
- Prepares and submits the state budget to the General Assembly
- Appoints heads of state agencies and boards
- Serves as Commander-in-Chief of the Indiana National Guard
- Issues executive orders and proclamations
- Grants pardons and clemency
- Calls special sessions of the General Assembly

Indiana State Government Structure

Executive Branch
Indiana's executive branch includes the Governor, Lieutenant Governor, and numerous state agencies and departments including:
- Indiana Department of Revenue
- Indiana Bureau of Motor Vehicles
- Indiana Department of Education
- Indiana Department of Health
- Indiana State Police
- Indiana Department of Transportation
- Indiana Department of Environmental Management
- Indiana Department of Natural Resources
- Indiana Family and Social Services Administration

Legislative Branch: Indiana General Assembly
The Indiana General Assembly is the state legislature, consisting of two chambers:
- Indiana Senate: 50 senators, serve 4-year terms
- Indiana House of Representatives: 100 representatives, serve 2-year terms
The General Assembly meets in regular session beginning in January each year.

Judicial Branch
Indiana's court system includes:
- Indiana Supreme Court (highest court, 5 justices)
- Indiana Court of Appeals (15 judges)
- Indiana Tax Court
- Circuit Courts and Superior Courts in each county

Indiana State Facts
- State capital: Indianapolis
- Statehood: December 11, 1816 (19th state)
- State nickname: "Hoosier State"
- State motto: "Crossroads of America"
- State bird: Cardinal
- State tree: Tulip tree (yellow poplar)
- State flower: Peony
- Largest city: Indianapolis

Indiana U.S. Congressional Delegation
Indiana is represented in the U.S. Congress by two U.S. Senators and nine U.S. Representatives (based on congressional districts). Indiana's U.S. Senators serve six-year terms.

State Fiscal Year
Indiana's fiscal year runs from July 1 to June 30. The Indiana General Assembly passes a biennial budget (two-year budget) each odd-numbered year.
