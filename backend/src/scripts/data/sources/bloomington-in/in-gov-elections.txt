Indiana Election Division - Official Elections Website

Indiana Elections

The Indiana Election Division is a bipartisan agency within the Indiana Secretary of State's office that administers Indiana's election laws.

Indiana Voter Registration
- Indiana residents can register to vote online at indianavoters.in.gov
- Voter registration deadline: 29 days before an election
- Indiana has automatic voter registration through the Bureau of Motor Vehicles (BMV)
- Same-day registration is not available in Indiana

Voting in Indiana
- Polls in Indiana are open from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM local time on Election Day
- Indiana uses paper ballots with optical scan counting machines
- Absentee voting is available (by mail or in-person at county clerk's office)
- Indiana requires a photo ID to vote (Indiana Voter ID law)
- Accepted photo IDs include Indiana driver's license, state ID, U.S. passport, military ID, and others

Indiana Primary Elections
- Indiana holds primary elections in May of even-numbered years
- Registered voters can participate in either party's primary (open primary for unaffiliated voters)

Indiana General Elections
- General elections are held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in November in even-numbered years

Monroe County Elections Administration
- Monroe County elections are administered by the Monroe County Clerk
- Monroe County has numerous voting precincts throughout the county
- Bloomington has multiple polling locations serving different precincts
- Monroe County offers vote centers â€” registered voters can vote at any vote center in the county

Indiana U.S. Congressional Elections
- Indiana elects two U.S. Senators and nine U.S. Representatives
- U.S. Senators serve 6-year terms; U.S. Representatives serve 2-year terms

Indiana State Elections
- Governor and other statewide offices are elected in even-numbered years
- Indiana state legislators are elected every 2 years (House) or 4 years (Senate)
- Indiana judges are selected through merit selection; retention elections determine continued service

Bloomington City Elections
- Bloomington city elections occur in odd-numbered years
- Bloomington Mayor elected every 4 years
- Bloomington City Council members elected every 4 years
- Bloomington City Clerk elected every 4 years
- Bloomington uses a partisan primary election system for city races

Monroe County Board of Commissioners and County Council
- Monroe County Commissioners and County Council members are elected in even-numbered years
- Monroe County Commissioner terms are 4 years
- Monroe County Council members serve 4-year terms

Campaign Finance
- Indiana campaign finance reports are filed with the Indiana Election Division
- Candidates must report contributions and expenditures according to Indiana law

Indiana Secretary of State
The Indiana Secretary of State oversees elections in Indiana through the Indiana Election Division. The Secretary of State is an elected position serving a four-year term.
