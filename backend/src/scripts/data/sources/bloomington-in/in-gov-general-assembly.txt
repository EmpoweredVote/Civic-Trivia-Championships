Indiana General Assembly - Official Legislative Website

Indiana General Assembly

The Indiana General Assembly is the state legislature of Indiana, consisting of the Indiana Senate and the Indiana House of Representatives.

Indiana Senate
- 50 senators
- Senators serve 4-year terms
- Senate is presided over by the Lieutenant Governor
- Senate President Pro Tempore elected by senators
- Senate meets in the Indiana Statehouse in Indianapolis

Indiana House of Representatives
- 100 representatives
- Representatives serve 2-year terms
- Speaker of the House elected by representatives
- House meets in the Indiana Statehouse in Indianapolis

Legislative Session
The Indiana General Assembly meets in regular session beginning on the second Tuesday after January 1 in odd-numbered years and the first Tuesday after January 1 in even-numbered years. Short sessions (even years) typically end by March 14; long sessions (odd years) typically end by April 29.

Indiana Statehouse
The Indiana Statehouse, located in Indianapolis, is home to the Indiana General Assembly, the Governor's office, and the Indiana Supreme Court. It was completed in 1888.

Legislative Process in Indiana
- Bills can be introduced in either chamber
- Bills must pass both chambers in identical form
- Governor can sign or veto passed legislation
- Legislature can override a veto with a simple majority in both chambers

Indiana's Legislative Districts
Indiana is divided into 50 Senate districts and 100 House districts. Bloomington is in Monroe County, which falls in multiple legislative districts.

Monroe County Legislative Representation
Monroe County residents are represented by multiple state legislators in both the Indiana Senate and Indiana House of Representatives. Bloomington's state legislative districts reflect the city's and county's population.

Key Legislative Committees
The Indiana General Assembly has numerous standing committees including:
- Ways and Means (House) / Appropriations (Senate) — budget and taxes
- Education — K-12 and higher education
- Judiciary — courts and criminal law
- Public Health — health policy
- Local Government — cities, towns, and counties
- Environmental Affairs — environment and natural resources

Indiana Laws and Statutes
Indiana's laws are compiled in the Indiana Code. The Indiana Administrative Code contains regulations adopted by state agencies. Both are maintained by the Indiana Legislative Services Agency.

Hoosier Roots
The origin of "Hoosier" — Indiana's nickname for its residents — is debated, but the term has been used since the early 19th century and is a source of state pride.
