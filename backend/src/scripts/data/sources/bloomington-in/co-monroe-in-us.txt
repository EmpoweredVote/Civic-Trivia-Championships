Monroe County, Indiana - Official Government Website

Monroe County Government Overview

Monroe County is a county in the south-central region of Indiana, United States. The county seat is Bloomington, Indiana. Monroe County was formed in 1818 and named after President James Monroe.

County Government Structure

Monroe County operates under the Indiana county government structure with three elected County Commissioners who serve as the chief executive body for the county.

Board of Commissioners
The Monroe County Board of Commissioners consists of three members elected from three districts. The Commissioners are responsible for:
- Overseeing county departments and offices
- Adopting the annual county budget
- Managing county property and facilities
- Setting county policy and ordinances

County Council
The Monroe County Council is the fiscal body of county government, consisting of seven members â€” four elected from districts and three elected at-large. The Council:
- Adopts the county budget
- Sets tax rates
- Approves expenditures
- Controls county finances

Elected Officials
Monroe County has numerous elected offices including:
- County Auditor (maintains county financial records)
- County Treasurer (collects taxes and manages county funds)
- County Assessor (determines property values for tax purposes)
- County Recorder (maintains land records and deeds)
- County Clerk (maintains court records and administers elections)
- County Sheriff (law enforcement and jail management)
- County Prosecutor (prosecutes criminal cases)
- County Surveyor (maintains land surveys and drainage)
- County Coroner (investigates unexplained deaths)
- County Superintendent of Schools (oversees county school system)

County Courts
Monroe County has a Circuit Court, Superior Courts, and a Probate Court. Indiana courts are organized with the Indiana Supreme Court at the top.

County Services
Monroe County provides numerous services to residents including:
- Monroe County Sheriff's Office (law enforcement)
- Monroe County Public Library (multiple branches serving the county)
- Monroe County Solid Waste Management District
- Monroe County Health Department
- Monroe County Highway Department (road maintenance)
- Monroe County Emergency Management
- Monroe County Animal Care and Control
- Monroe County Assessor's Office (property assessment)
- Monroe County Convention and Visitors Bureau

Population and Demographics
Monroe County has a population of approximately 148,000 residents. Bloomington, the county seat, is the largest city and home to Indiana University. The county includes several townships.

Indiana University
Indiana University's main campus is located in Bloomington, making Monroe County home to one of Indiana's largest universities. IU has a significant civic and economic impact on the county.

Monroe County Public Library
The Monroe County Public Library system serves residents of Bloomington and Monroe County with a main branch and satellite locations. The library offers books, digital resources, community programs, and more.

Monroe County History
Monroe County was established by the Indiana General Assembly on November 14, 1818. The county was named for President James Monroe. Bloomington was established as the county seat in 1818.

Voting and Elections in Monroe County
Monroe County elections are administered by the Monroe County Clerk's Office in conjunction with the Indiana Election Division. Monroe County has multiple voting precincts. Voters can check their registration and polling place through the Indiana Voter Registration Portal (indianavoters.in.gov).
